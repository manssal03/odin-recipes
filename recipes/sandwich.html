Chopped Greek Chicken Sandwich

Greek Chicken Sandwich

This chopped Greek chicken sandwich makes life easy when you can't decide between a Greek salad and a chicken sandwich. Pile up the components of a salad, chop, mix, and pack them into a sandwich roll, and you will absolutely love the result.

Ingredients

(1 serving)

  • 1 sandwich roll
  • 6 ounces cooked chicken
  • 1/4 cup thinly sliced green pepper
  • 4 grape tomatoes, thinly sliced, or more to taste
  • 4 slices English cucumber, or more to taste
  • 6 pitted Greek olives, halved, or more to taste
  • 3 thin slices red onion, or more to taste
  • 1 cup thickly sliced hearts of Romaine
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 pinch cayenne pepper, or to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried oregano, or to taste
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/2 lemon, juiced
  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Cut sandwich roll 3/4 of the way in, and if desired, remove some of the crumb from the inside to make more room for filling.
  3. Pile up chicken, green pepper, tomatoes, cucumber, olives, and onion. Season with salt, black pepper, cayenne, and oregano, and then use a cleaver or large knife to chop everything into about 1/2-inch pieces.
  4. Add Romaine, and chop again until everything into about 1/4-inch pieces, or as fine as you want.
  5. Top with feta, lemon juice, and olive oil. Perform one last, brief chopping until everything is evenly mixed. Taste and adjust seasoning.
  6. Transfer filling into roll, and serve immediately.
